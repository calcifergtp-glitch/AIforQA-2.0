Cookie Policy

Last updated: March 7, 2026

This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, which ones we use on AI for Pharmaceutical QA, and how you can control them. By using this website, you consent to the use of cookies as described below.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They allow the site (or third-party services) to recognize your browser and remember certain information. Cookies do not contain executable code and cannot access other files on your device.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the website to function. They do not store personally identifiable information and cannot be disabled without affecting core site functionality. At this time, we do not use any strictly necessary session cookies beyond what your browser handles natively.

Analytics Cookies

We may use analytics services such as Google Analytics to understand how visitors interact with the Site. These cookies collect anonymous information such as:

  • Number of visitors and page views
  • Time spent on pages
  • Traffic sources (how you found the site)
  • General geographic location (country or region)

This data is aggregated and does not identify individual users. Google Analytics may set cookies including _ga, _gid, and _gat that typically expire within 2 years, 24 hours, and 1 minute respectively.

Advertising Cookies

If advertising is enabled on this Site (for example, through Google AdSense), ad networks may set cookies to show you relevant ads based on your browsing history and interests. These cookies are set by third parties and are subject to their own privacy and cookie policies.

Functionality Cookies

These cookies remember your preferences (such as theme or region settings) to improve your experience on future visits. We may use these if functionality features are added in the future.

3. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies on this Site are set by third-party services we use. We do not control these cookies. Common third-party cookie sources may include:

  • Google Analytics — Google Privacy Policy
  • Google AdSense — Google Advertising Policies
  • Affiliate networks — governed by each network's own cookie and privacy policy

4. How to Control Cookies

You have several options to control or delete cookies:

  • Browser settings: Most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies through their settings menu. Refer to your browser's help documentation for instructions.
  • Google Analytics opt-out: Install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.
  • Advertising opt-out: Visit optout.aboutads.info or optout.networkadvertising.org to opt out of interest-based advertising.

Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of this website or third-party services embedded within it.

5. Cookie Consent

By continuing to use this Site, you consent to the placement of cookies as described in this policy. Where legally required, we will provide a cookie consent banner to collect your explicit agreement before placing non-essential cookies.
